# Target-Journal Shortlist

Verified on 2026-07-17 from official journal or publisher pages. Limits and fees
change; every item should be rechecked immediately before submission.

## Ranked shortlist

### 1. Journal of Pathology Informatics — strong fit

- **Publisher:** Elsevier on behalf of the Association for Pathology Informatics.
- **Suitable article type:** Research Article; a Technical Note is a secondary option
  because the official scope explicitly includes validation experience, lessons
  learned, and unsuccessful implementation.
- **Scope fit:** Direct. The paper is about pathology informatics, model validation,
  confidence reliability, and hospital-domain evaluation.
- **Why it fits:** The negative GroupDRO result is paired with a pathology-specific
  validation and governance contribution. The audience is likely to value the
  one-shot protocol, reliability audit, and honest external-hospital reversal.
- **Main weaknesses:** Patch-level rather than WSI/patient-level evaluation; one
  benchmark; limited architecture and DG-method breadth.
- **Current limits located:** Abstract no more than 250 words; 1–7 keywords. No
  explicit main-text or display-item cap was located in the official guide.
- **Reference style:** Numbered AMA-style references in the final journal format.
- **Open access:** Fully open access.
- **APC:** A current amount was not displayed in the retrieved official guide; verify
  in Elsevier's journal pricing tool at submission.
- **Data/code policy:** Research-data deposit/linking is encouraged; authors should
  provide or explain access to supporting data.
- **Reporting:** Include ethics, funding, competing-interest, authorship, data, and
  generative-AI disclosures required by the current guide. CLAIM is a sensible
  voluntary checklist for this paper.
- **Preprints:** Permitted and not treated as prior publication.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Realistic specialist target. Editorial risk is mainly
  whether the study is seen as too benchmark-centric rather than pathology-informatics
  validation work.
- **Official guide:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-pathology-informatics/publish/guide-for-authors

### 2. Journal of Medical Imaging — strong-to-moderate fit

- **Publisher:** SPIE.
- **Suitable article type:** Research Paper.
- **Scope fit:** The journal explicitly covers medical-imaging methods and
  applications, including digital and computational pathology.
- **Why it fits:** The work provides a controlled evaluation of imaging-model
  robustness, calibration, and transportability with a reproducible final-test
  protocol.
- **Main weaknesses:** The paper does not propose a new imaging algorithm and is
  patch-level. The editorial case must foreground evaluation methodology and
  reliability rather than claim method novelty.
- **Current limits located:** The official author page should be checked in the live
  submission system; the accessible guidance supports an approximately 250-word
  abstract, but no reliable current main-text, display-item, or APC limit was exposed
  in the retrieved page.
- **Reference style:** SPIE numerical style.
- **Open access:** Hybrid/open-access options are available through SPIE.
- **APC:** Not recorded because a current journal-specific amount was not verifiable
  from the retrieved official page.
- **Data/code policy:** State code, model, and data access terms explicitly; current
  journal editor guidance emphasizes accessible core resources for reproducibility.
- **Reporting:** CLAIM should accompany the submission.
- **Preprints:** Permitted under SPIE journal policy.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Higher technical-imaging bar than JPI; moderate risk
  that protocol novelty is judged insufficient without a new algorithm.
- **Official guide:** https://www.spiedigitallibrary.org/journals/journal-of-medical-imaging/author-guidelines

### 3. PLOS ONE — strong methodological fallback

- **Publisher:** Public Library of Science.
- **Suitable article type:** Research Article.
- **Scope fit:** Broad original research evaluated for rigor and validity rather than
  perceived significance. Negative and replication-like findings are compatible with
  that editorial model.
- **Why it fits:** The complete protocol, preserved unfavorable result, reproducibility
  record, and transparent limitations align well with PLOS ONE's criteria.
- **Main weaknesses:** Less pathology-specific audience; strong data/code availability
  expectations; reviewers may request broader confidence intervals or patient/slide
  clustering discussion.
- **Current limits:** No manuscript word, figure, or supporting-information limit;
  abstract no more than 300 words.
- **Reference style:** Vancouver/ICMJE numerical style.
- **Open access:** Fully open access under a Creative Commons license.
- **Current APC:** USD 2,477 for all other articles, subject to submission-date change
  and institutional/Research4Life assistance.
- **Data/code policy:** Strong data-availability requirement; data underlying reported
  findings must be available unless a justified restriction applies.
- **Reporting:** Use CLAIM/TRIPOD+AI as applicable and provide the relevant checklist.
- **Preprints:** Permitted and encouraged.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Lower scope-rejection risk, but broad readership means
  the protocol contribution must be explained without pathology-specific assumptions.
- **Official guidelines:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/s/submission-guidelines
- **Official fees:** https://plos.org/fees/

### 4. Scientific Reports — moderate fit

- **Publisher:** Springer Nature.
- **Suitable article type:** Article.
- **Scope fit:** Broad natural-science and medicine scope with technical-validity
  review.
- **Why it fits:** The work is complete, reproducible, and scientifically informative
  despite a negative primary-model result.
- **Main weaknesses:** The main-text recommendation is substantially shorter than the
  current draft; single-benchmark and patch-level scope may be viewed as incremental.
- **Current limits:** Main text recommended at no more than 4,500 words excluding
  abstract, methods, references, and legends; title no more than 20 words; abstract no
  more than 200 words; ideally no more than 11 typeset pages.
- **Reference style:** Nature numerical style.
- **Open access:** Fully open access.
- **Current APC:** The official Nature Portfolio page states Scientific Reports APCs
  start at EUR 2,490; the exact local-currency amount must be checked at submission.
- **Data/code policy:** Mandatory data-availability statement and strong
  reproducibility expectations.
- **Reporting:** CLAIM is advisable; all methods and limitations must remain explicit.
- **Preprints:** Permitted under Springer Nature policy.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Moderate scope fit but higher editorial risk than PLOS
  ONE because the contribution may be judged too narrow.
- **Official guidelines:** https://www.nature.com/srep/author-instructions/submission-guidelines

### 5. BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making — moderate fit

- **Publisher:** Springer Nature/BMC.
- **Suitable article type:** Research Article.
- **Scope fit:** Medical informatics, machine learning, decision support, model
  evaluation, and health-information methods.
- **Why it fits:** The protocol and reliability findings can be framed as medical-AI
  evaluation and decision-policy transportability.
- **Main weaknesses:** Pathology image classification is less central to the journal
  than clinical informatics and decision making. There is no clinical decision or
  patient-level outcome.
- **Current limits:** Structured abstract up to 350 words and 3–10 keywords; no
  explicit research-article main-text cap was located on the official page.
- **Reference style:** Vancouver numerical style.
- **Open access:** Fully open access.
- **Current APC:** Official pricing must be rechecked at submission; Springer Nature
  country-tier and institutional agreements may alter the charge.
- **Data/code policy:** A data-availability statement is required.
- **Reporting:** TRIPOD+AI/CLAIM should be mapped explicitly.
- **Preprints:** Permitted under BMC policy.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Moderate; reviewers may ask for broader clinical
  decision relevance.
- **Official aims:** https://link.springer.com/journal/12911/aims-and-scope
- **Official article guide:** https://link.springer.com/journal/12911/submission-guidelines/research-article

### 6. Journal of Imaging Informatics in Medicine — moderate fit

- **Publisher:** Springer Nature; official journal of the Society for Imaging
  Informatics in Medicine.
- **Suitable article type:** Original Research or Technical/Experience-oriented
  submission, subject to current editorial categories.
- **Scope fit:** Imaging informatics, AI/ML, quality assurance, safety, bias, and
  implementation/validation.
- **Why it fits:** The reliability and safety framing is relevant, especially the
  high-confidence false-negative and one-shot validation components.
- **Main weaknesses:** The journal prioritizes imaging-informatics implementation and
  may consider a standalone benchmark comparison insufficiently translational.
- **Current limits:** Official Springer guidance uses a 150–250-word abstract and 4–6
  keywords; no main-text limit was located.
- **Reference style:** Springer numerical style.
- **Open access:** Hybrid. Subscription publication may have no APC; optional open
  access has a journal-specific charge that must be checked at submission.
- **Data/code policy:** Springer research-data policy; provide availability statements
  and repository details.
- **Preprints:** Permitted under Springer Nature policy.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** Moderate-to-high editorial fit risk due to patch-level
  and non-implementation scope.
- **Official aims:** https://link.springer.com/journal/10278/aims-and-scope
- **Official guide:** https://link.springer.com/journal/10278/submission-guidelines

### 7. Artificial Intelligence in Medicine — weak/high-risk fit

- **Publisher:** Elsevier.
- **Suitable article type:** Original Research only if editors accept the evaluation
  methodology as a substantive methodological contribution.
- **Scope fit:** Medical AI and methodological evaluation are relevant.
- **Why it might fit:** The predeclared one-shot evaluation and reliability package
  address rigorous medical-AI assessment.
- **Main weakness:** The official guide explicitly says that mere application of
  established algorithms to medical data is not considered original research of
  interest. This manuscript introduces no new AI method.
- **Current limits:** Abstract no more than 250 words; 1–7 keywords. No explicit
  main-text cap was located.
- **Reference style:** Elsevier numerical style.
- **Open access:** Hybrid.
- **APC:** Not recorded because a current amount was not located in the retrieved
  official guide.
- **Data/code policy:** Follow Elsevier's research-data policy and explain any access
  limits.
- **Preprints:** Permitted under Elsevier policy.
- **Prestige-risk assessment:** High desk-rejection risk. Do not submit before the
  better-fit venues unless the framing is materially recast around evaluation
  methodology.
- **Official guide:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/artificial-intelligence-in-medicine/publish/guide-for-authors

## Recommended order

1. Journal of Pathology Informatics
2. Journal of Medical Imaging
3. PLOS ONE
4. Scientific Reports
5. BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making
6. Journal of Imaging Informatics in Medicine
7. Artificial Intelligence in Medicine

The order prioritizes audience and methodological fit over impact-factor chasing.
